Scenario:
A common situation where poor digital communication causes real problems is when a student applies for an internship using an unprofessional email address (e.g., "dark_ninja99@email.com") and writes the email body using casual text-speak (like "plz find attached my resume, thx"). Because the email lacks a clear subject line, a proper greeting, and professional tone, the company's spam filter might block it, or the HR manager might instantly delete it, assuming the candidate lacks basic professional skills.
What Could Have Been Done Differently:
The student should have used their official college email or a simple firstname.lastname format. They needed a clear subject line like "Application for Summer Internship - [Name]". Furthermore, they should have used a formal salutation ("Dear Hiring Manager,"), written in full sentences with proper grammar, and included a professional sign-off at the bottom. Taking five extra minutes to format the email correctly would have completely changed the employer's first impression.
